sally of our alley of all the girls in our town there is none like pretty sally she is the darling of my heart and lives in our alley there is never a lady in the land is half so sweet as sally she is the darling of my heart and lives in our alley her father he makes cabbage nets and in the streets does cry them her mother she sells laces long to all who choose to buy them but sure such folks could never beget so sweet a girl as sally she is the darling etc when sallys by i leave my work i love her so sincerely my master comes like any turk and bangs me most severely but let him bang his belly full i will bear it all for sally she is etc of all the days that is in the week i dearly love but one day and that is the day that comes between a saturday and monday o then i am dressed in all my best to walk abroad with sally she is etc my master carries me to church and often i am blamed because i leave him in the lurch as soon as text is named i leave the church in sermon time and slink away to sally she is etc when christmas comes about again o then i shall have money i will hoard it up and box and all i will give it to my sally and would it were a thousand pounds i would give it all to sally she is etc my master and the neighbors all make game of me and sally and but for her i would better be a slave and row a galley but when my seven long years are out o then i will marry sally o then well wed and then well bed but not in our alley printed and sold by j davenport no   little chatherinestreet strand london
